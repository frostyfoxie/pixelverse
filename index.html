Ever wondered how we all came to exist?
Don't worry I got ya, lemme tell you
from the very beginning hahaha!
No space. No time. No laws. No direction.
Not darkness—because darkness needs space to exist.
There was no “before.”
Because time itself hadn’t begun.
And then—
something changed.
The Big Bang
Not an explosion in space, but the appearance of space itself.
Inflation
A violent stretching of reality.
Energy filled everything.
Temperature beyond comprehension.
The First Seconds
As it expanded and cooled, tiny fluctuations became everything.
Particles blinked into existence and vanished.
Then some stayed, forming protons and neutrons.
The First Atoms
The universe cooled enough for protons to capture electrons.
The first atoms formed: Hydrogen and Helium.
Simple, light, and everywhere.
And then—
nothing much happened… for millions of years.
The Long Darkness
No stars or galaxies, just a vast, expanding fog of atoms.
Gravity was at work—quietly, constantly—
pulling matter into clumps as structure began to emerge.
The First Stars
Gravity pulled regions denser, causing clouds to collapse.
Then, fusion ignited. The first stars were born,
and light finally entered the universe.
Cosmic Alchemy
Inside those stars, something incredible happened.
Hydrogen fused into helium.
Helium into carbon. Carbon into heavier elements.
The universe learned complexity.
Every atom in your body—carbon, oxygen, nitrogen—
was forged in the heart of a star.
Death of Stars
But stars don’t last forever.
When they run out of fuel, they collapse.
Some explode violently in Supernovae.
In those explosions, the heaviest elements are formed.
Gold. Iron. Calcium.
Then, scattered like seeds across space.
Galaxies Form
Over billions of years, matter organized into galaxies.
Billions of stars, all bound together by gravity.
And one of them—just one—
would come to matter to you.
A New Beginning
Amidst the billions of stars, a small cloud of dust began to collapse.
Our Sun was born—a modest star in the suburbs of the Milky Way.
Accretion
Leftover debris collided and merged, forming the planets.
Among them was a small, rocky world, positioned just right.
Early Earth
A chaotic landscape of molten rock and constant bombardment.
Yet, as it cooled, something miraculous happened...
but that is a story for another time.
The Origin of Life
Somewhere in those oceans, molecules began to organize.
Not alive—yet. But close.
Then, somehow—something began to replicate.
Imperfect copies. Small errors.
That was enough. Evolution had begun.
Billions of Years of Trial
Life didn’t aim for perfection. It just kept going.
Most attempts failed.
Extinction after extinction.
But some survived.
And those survivors changed.
Complexity
Cells became more advanced. They learned to cooperate.
Multicellular life appeared.
Eyes. Limbs. Movement.
The world filled with strange forms.
Dinosaurs
For millions of years, they dominated Earth.
Massive. Adapted. Successful.
They ruled longer than humans have existed.
The Impact
Then—a rock from space.
Fast. Silent. Unstoppable.
It hit.
Firestorms. Darkness. Collapse.
Most life disappeared.
Survival
But not all.
Small creatures endured.
Hidden. Adaptive. Resilient.
They inherited the world.
Mammals Rise
They grew. Diversified. Experimented.
Among them— primates.
Humans
One branch evolved something unusual.
Awareness. The ability to think… about thinking.
To question. To imagine.
To understand the universe— even partially.
